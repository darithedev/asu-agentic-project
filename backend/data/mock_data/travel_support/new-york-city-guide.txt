NEW YORK CITY TRAVEL GUIDE

Everything you need to know for your visit to the Big Apple.

WHEN TO VISIT
Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer the best weather. Summer can be hot and humid but has many outdoor events. Winter is cold but magical during the holidays with fewer crowds.

TOP ATTRACTIONS
1. Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island: Symbolic icon accessible by ferry. Book ferry tickets online.
2. Central Park: 843-acre park with lakes, gardens, and attractions. Free admission.
3. Empire State Building: Classic observation deck with 360-degree views. Skip-the-line tickets recommended.
4. Times Square: The heart of Manhattan, best visited in evening for neon lights.
5. Metropolitan Museum of Art: One of world's largest art museums. Suggested donation entry.
6. Brooklyn Bridge: Walk across this iconic bridge for stunning skyline views.

GETTING AROUND
The subway system is extensive and affordable ($2.75 per ride). Purchase a MetroCard or use contactless payment. Yellow taxis are everywhere. Walking is the best way to experience different neighborhoods.

NEIGHBORHOODS TO EXPLORE
- Manhattan: Financial District, SoHo, Greenwich Village, Upper East/West Sides
- Brooklyn: Williamsburg, DUMBO, Brooklyn Heights
- Queens: Flushing (great food), Astoria
- The Bronx: Yankee Stadium, Bronx Zoo

ACCOMMODATION
Hotels in Manhattan are expensive. Consider staying in Brooklyn or Queens for better value while remaining accessible. Airbnb options are available but check local regulations.

DINING
NYC is a food paradise. Try pizza slices, bagels with cream cheese, street food, and diverse ethnic cuisines. Reservations essential for popular restaurants. Don't miss a classic New York deli.

SHOPPING
Fifth Avenue for luxury, SoHo for trendy boutiques, Times Square for souvenirs. Many neighborhoods have unique shopping districts.

TIPS
- Tipping is expected (15-20% at restaurants, $1-2 per drink at bars)
- NYC can be expensive - budget accordingly
- Wear comfortable shoes - you'll walk a lot
- Many attractions offer timed entry - book in advance
- Subway runs 24/7 but less frequent late night
- Be street smart, especially in crowded tourist areas
